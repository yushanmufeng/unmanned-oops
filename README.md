# Unmanned-Oops

**A systemic archive documenting operational and reliability failures resulting from aggressive AI-driven workforce displacement across industries.**

---

### Project Vision
As AI automation is aggressively integrated into enterprise environments, many human roles are being replaced. This repository serves as a fact-based ledger to objectively track the real-world operational costs of this transition. We focus on how business logic, reliability, and governance boundaries degrade when critical human oversight and professional judgment are removed.

### Live Site
You can browse the archive online at:  
[Unmanned Oops — Live Archive](https://yushanmufeng.github.io/unmanned-oops/)

### Archive Categories

* **Systemic Failures**
  Focuses on operational degradation, increased Mean Time To Recovery (MTTR), and loss of quality control in critical business processes caused by replacing human expertise with AI automation.

* **Individual Impacts**
  Focuses on the unintended consequences of AI governance on individuals, including algorithmic overreach, misclassification of workflows, or erroneous deletion and corruption of data.

### About This Project
This project is currently in the **archiving and documentation phase**. We aim to explore the risk boundaries of AI in corporate governance through real-world cases. All records are archived based on publicly available information gathered from the internet, providing an objective "front-line" perspective rather than final verdicts. If you spot inaccuracies or have better primary sources, corrections and suggestions are very welcome.

### Contributing
We warmly welcome contributions of new cases to this archive. Cases are stored as JSON files under `public/content/YYYY/MM.json`, where each month file contains an array of entries with `title`, `description`, `time`, `url`, and `tags`. Please base submissions on publicly verifiable sources, keep the wording factual and neutral in tone, and follow the existing structure and style of the archive.
